Pharmacology Notes

1. General Pharmacology

  • Pharmacokinetics – Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion (ADME)
  • Pharmacodynamics – Mechanism of drug action
  • Drug interactions – Synergism, Antagonism
  • Adverse drug reactions – Type A (dose-related), Type B (idiosyncratic)

2. Autonomic Drugs

  • Sympathomimetics – e.g., Epinephrine
  • Sympatholytics – e.g., Propranolol
  • Parasympathomimetics – e.g., Pilocarpine
  • Parasympatholytics – e.g., Atropine

3. Cardiovascular Drugs

  • Antihypertensives – ACE inhibitors, Beta-blockers, Diuretics
  • Antianginal – Nitrates, Calcium channel blockers
  • Antiarrhythmics – Class I–IV drugs
  • Anticoagulants – Heparin, Warfarin

4. Antibiotics & Antimicrobials

  • Beta-lactams – Penicillins, Cephalosporins
  • Aminoglycosides – Gentamicin
  • Macrolides – Erythromycin
  • Fluoroquinolones – Ciprofloxacin
  • Antifungals – Amphotericin B, Azoles

5. CNS Drugs

  • Analgesics – Opioids, NSAIDs
  • Antiepileptics – Phenytoin, Valproate
  • Antidepressants – SSRIs, TCAs
  • Antipsychotics – Typical and Atypical
  • Parkinson’s – Levodopa, Dopamine agonists

6. Endocrine Drugs

  • Insulin and oral hypoglycemics – Metformin, Sulfonylureas
  • Thyroid – Levothyroxine
  • Adrenal – Corticosteroids
