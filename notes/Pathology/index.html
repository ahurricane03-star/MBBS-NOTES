Pathology Notes

1. General Pathology

  • Cell Injury: Reversible and Irreversible
  • Cell Adaptation: Hypertrophy, Hyperplasia, Atrophy, Metaplasia
  • Necrosis: Coagulative, Liquefactive, Caseous, Fat, Fibrinoid
  • Apoptosis – programmed cell death
  • Inflammation:
    • Acute – neutrophil predominant
    • Chronic – lymphocytes and macrophages
  • Repair & Healing – regeneration, fibrosis

2. Hematology

  • Anemias:
    • Microcytic – Iron deficiency
    • Macrocytic – Vitamin B12 / Folate deficiency
    • Normocytic – Hemolytic, Acute blood loss
  • Leukemias – acute vs chronic, lymphoid vs myeloid
  • Coagulation disorders – Hemophilia A/B, Vitamin K deficiency

3. Systemic Pathology

  • Cardiovascular:
    • Atherosclerosis, Myocardial Infarction
  • Respiratory:
    • Pneumonia, Tuberculosis
  • Gastrointestinal:
    • Peptic ulcer, Hepatitis
  • Renal:
    • Glomerulonephritis, Acute tubular necrosis

4. Neoplasia

  • Benign vs Malignant tumors
  • Carcinogenesis – genetic mutations, oncogenes, tumor suppressor genes
  • Common tumors – carcinoma, sarcoma, lymphoma

5. Infection & Immunity

  • Bacterial, Viral, Fungal, Parasitic infections
  • Immune response – innate and adaptive
  • Hypersensitivity reactions – Type I to IV
