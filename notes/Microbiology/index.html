Baby Microbiology Notes

1. Bacteria

  • Prokaryotic, unicellular
  • Shapes: Cocci (round), Bacilli (rod), Spirilla (spiral)
  • Gram Stain: Gram + (purple), Gram - (pink)
  • Common bacteria:
    • Staphylococcus aureus – skin infections
    • Escherichia coli – UTI, gut infections
    • Mycobacterium tuberculosis – TB

2. Viruses

  • Obligate intracellular parasites
  • Genetic material: DNA or RNA
  • Examples:
    • Influenza virus – flu
    • HIV – AIDS
    • Hepatitis B virus – liver infection

3. Fungi

  • Eukaryotic, cell wall with chitin
  • Yeast: unicellular (e.g., Candida)
  • Mold: multicellular (e.g., Aspergillus)

4. Parasites

  • Protozoa – unicellular (e.g., Plasmodium → malaria)
  • Helminths – multicellular worms (e.g., Ascaris, Taenia)

5. Sterilization & Disinfection

  • Autoclaving – steam under pressure
  • Dry heat – hot air oven
  • Chemical disinfectants – alcohol, bleach

6. Immunology Basics

  • Innate immunity – first line defense
  • Adaptive immunity – B cells (antibodies), T cells (cell-mediated)
  • Vaccines – stimulate immunity without disease
